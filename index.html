Description

Red loam soil is a reddish-brown soil that is sandy-loamy in texture. It is slightly acidic to neutral and relatively fertile due to moderate levels of nutrients. Red loam soils have good drainage and moderate water-holding capacity.

They are generally well aerated and support a wide variety of crops.

Soil Properties of Red Loam Soil

  1. Texture: Reddish-brown, sandy-loamy
  2. Drainage: Well drained
  3. Water-holding capacity: Moderate
  4. Soil reaction (pH): Slightly acidic to neutral (≈6.0–6.8)
  5. Fertility status: Moderate; better in nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium
  6. Organic matter content: Medium to high
  7. Iron and aluminium oxides: Moderate
  8. Groundwater table: Deep to medium
  9. Soil colour: Reddish-brown to red

Crops Cultivated

  • Paddy, sugarcane, cotton, groundnut, banana, vegetable crops, pulses
